# Product Guidelines: Riffl

## Purpose
This document defines the branding, communication style, and user experience (UX) principles for Riffl. It serves as a guide for development and documentation to ensure a consistent and high-quality user experience.

## Communication Style & Tone
- **Academic & Thorough:** Documentation, in-app messages, and labels should be precise, technically accurate, and comprehensive. We value depth and clarity, ensuring that every feature and parameter is fully documented with its underlying logic and behavior.
- **Modern & Sleek:** The aesthetic tone is contemporary, clean, and forward-looking. While Riffl draws inspiration from the history of tracking, its branding and visual style are firmly rooted in modern music production and software design.

## User Experience (UX) Principles
- **Clutter-Free Information Density:** We aim for a "sleek and simple" initial experience that provides a wealth of information without overwhelming the user.
- **Modal Accessibility:** To avoid a cluttered interface, the UI follows a "modal" philosophy. High-level features and views are easily accessible, but deep dives into specific parameters or settings are handled through focused, non-intrusive modes or overlays.
- **Progressive Disclosure:** Complexity is hidden until it is explicitly requested. The user can start making sound immediately, and then progressively explore more advanced features like scripting, automation, and sound design.
- **Efficiency Through Clarity:** While we prioritize a simple initial experience, we never compromise on the power and speed of the interface. Keyboard-driven workflows and clear visual hierarchy are paramount.

## Documentation & Guidance Priorities
- **Tutorial-Driven Onboarding:** Provide clear, step-by-step guides to help new users produce their first sounds and patterns as quickly as possible.
- **Reference-Heavy Depth:** Maintain a comprehensive technical reference for all aspects of the audio engine, TUI components, and scripting API.
- **Code-First Approach:** Highlighting the scripting and automation possibilities is a core priority. Documentation should include extensive examples and patterns for algorithmic composition.
- **Unified Knowledge Base:** Ensure that tutorials, references, and code examples are tightly integrated, allowing users to move seamlessly between learning and building.
